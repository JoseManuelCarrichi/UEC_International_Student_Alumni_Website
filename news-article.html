UEC Alumna Receives Prestigious Engineering Award

Published on October 15, 2024 by The Alumni Office

Tokyo, Japan â€“ We are incredibly proud to announce that Maria Rodriguez, a 2015 graduate of the Master's program in Mechanical Engineering and Intelligent Systems, has been awarded the coveted "Global Innovator Award" for her groundbreaking work in assistive robotics.

Maria's research focuses on creating intuitive, human-robot interfaces that allow for more natural control of prosthetic limbs. Her work, which began as her master's thesis project at UEC, has since evolved into a full-fledged system that is poised to dramatically improve the quality of life for amputees worldwide.

"My time at UEC was transformative," Maria said in her acceptance speech. "The guidance from my professors and the collaborative environment with my peers gave me the foundation I needed to pursue this challenging but rewarding field. This award isn't just for me; it's for the entire UEC community that supported me."

The Global Innovator Award is one of the highest honors in the field of engineering, recognizing individuals whose work has the potential for significant global impact. Maria is currently the Chief Technology Officer at "Connective Bionics," a startup she co-founded in her home country of Spain.

We extend our heartfelt congratulations to Maria for this outstanding achievement. Her success is a testament to the talent and dedication nurtured at UEC, and she serves as a powerful inspiration to both current students and fellow alumni.
